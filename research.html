Research & future

Exploring the frontier of human–machine and physical systems

In parallel with our commercial platforms, Agnis Labs invests in long-horizon research areas that sit at the intersection of software, biology, and advanced mobility.

Human–machine interfaces

From neural signals to usable systems

We are interested in how neural activity and biological signals can inform new kinds of interfaces, control systems, and assistive technologies.

Cellular biology-inspired models

Studying how cellular processes and signaling pathways can inspire new computational abstractions for robust, adaptive systems.

Neural signal processing & BCI

Exploring brain–computer interface concepts, from signal processing and decoding to potential software architectures that could support future applications.

Advanced propulsion

Next-generation propulsion & gravity-defying concepts

Looking beyond current vehicles, we’re interested in how future propulsion and control systems might enable vehicles that operate against gravity in new ways.

Conceptual vehicle architectures

Thinking through how software, sensing, and control would need to evolve for vehicles that operate in regimes beyond conventional ground transport.

Energy & fuel system exploration

Early-stage exploration of how emerging energy and propulsion technologies might change the constraints for high-performance, gravity-defying vehicles.

Why this matters

Building for the next generation

This research is a long-term investment in the kinds of systems the next generation of engineers will build—across healthcare, neurotechnology, and advanced transportation.
